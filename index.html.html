Character Vision Sheet
Made by: Skrip Writer + Sutradara · Share with: Wardrobe, Actors, Publication.
What is this? A one-page cheat sheet per character that tells actors HOW to play the role, and tells Wardrobe + Makeup WHAT the character should look like. Everyone gets the same vision.
Malin Kundang
Main Character · Hero → Antagonist
PersonalityObedient → ambitious → ungrateful. Arc from soft boy to cold businessman.
Moves likeScene 1: gentle, shy. Scene 2–3: confident, stiff. Freeze slowly for curse.
Speaks likeNervous and stumbling around Roro. Defensive and cold with Mama in Scene 3.
LookScene 1: simple village shirt. Scene 2–3: neat business casual, clean shoes.
Key momentSlowly freezing as the curse hits — play this straight, not funny.
Mama
Supporting · Strict Mother · Secret Winner
PersonalityControlling but loves Malin deeply. Hides pain behind authority. Comic but tragic.
Moves likeFirm posture, hands on hips a lot. Slow dramatic walk when she enters in Scene 3.
Speaks likeShort, sharp sentences. Never explains herself. Deadpan delivery = funnier.
LookTraditional kebaya or batik wrap. Sensible shoes. Hair tied up. No jewelry.
Key momentEnding on the throne — sit slowly, smile peacefully. The audience should laugh AND feel something.
Roro Jonggrang
Supporting · Love Interest · Brave Escapee
PersonalityAppears vulnerable at first but is quietly strong. Not a damsel — she makes the plan.
Moves likeGraceful, measured. Slight tension in body when near Bandung. Relaxes around Malin.
Speaks likeSoft voice, deliberate. Pauses before important lines. Eye contact is her weapon.
LookElegant but not overdone. Soft colors (dusty pink or lilac). Neat hair. Modest jewelry.
Key momentConfronting Bandung in Scene 3 — stand tall, direct eye contact, zero fear.
Jamilah
Comic Relief · Dayang #2 · Scene Stealer
PersonalityDesperately wants a boyfriend. Says whatever she thinks. Socially unaware but warm-hearted.
Moves likeBig gestures. Leans in too close. Raises hand confidently for wrong things.
Speaks likeLoud, enthusiastic. Slightly bad timing. Delivers lines like she's proud of herself.
LookSlightly mismatched outfit — colors that don't quite go together. Fussy accessories.
Key moment"I am also available" — step forward with full confidence and zero shame.
Bandung Bondowoso
Antagonist · Controlling Fiancé
PersonalityNPD villain — obsessed with control, not love. Collapses when he loses power.
Moves likeTakes up space. Slow deliberate walk. Hand gestures like he's conducting. Never slouches.
Speaks likeLow voice, over-confident. Long pauses for dramatic effect. "Good." is said a lot.
LookSharp black or navy suit. Pocket square. Cufflinks if possible. Hair slicked back.
Key momentLearning he must go to Jin world — confusion then panic. Let the mask slip here.
Jinny & Jonny
Jins · Trickster Duo · Comedy Pair
PersonalityJinny is clever and slightly sarcastic. Jonny is goofy and always sleepy/hungry.
Moves likeAppear suddenly with flair. Mirror each other's poses. Speak in rhythm like a duo.
Speaks likeJinny: dry wit. Jonny: complaints. Together they finish each other's sentences.
LookMatching quirky outfits — same color, different details. Think: fantastical + funny. Gold/purple.
Key moment"Do we get snacks?" — Jonny completely serious. Jinny nods in agreement.
Bodyguard 1 & 2
Extra · Reaction Machine · Scene Padding
PersonalityTry very hard to be professional. Fail constantly. React to everything dramatically.
Moves likeMilitary posture at all times. Snap to attention whenever Bandung enters.
Speaks likeFormal and official... until something shocks them. Then normal human panic.
LookMatching outfits. Dark pants, same shirt or vest. Earpiece if available (or fake it).
Key moment"Even my instant noodles need three minutes" — deliver this completely deadpan.
Bujang
Supporting · Best Friend · Emotional Support
PersonalityLoyal, cheerful, slightly oblivious. Genuinely wants the best for Malin.
Moves likeRelaxed and bouncy. Leans on things. Casual energy in every scene.
Speaks likeWarm and encouraging. Whispers good advice at the worst times.
LookScene 1: casual kampung. Scene 2–3: smart-casual, but slightly less polished than Malin.
Key moment"Bro… breathe." — said with complete calm while Malin panics.

Master Cue Sheet
Made by: Stage Manager · Used during: rehearsal and show day.
What is a cue sheet? Every time the lighting changes, a sound plays, or the scene transitions — that is a "cue." The Stage Manager calls each cue during the show by saying "Sound 3… GO" or "Light 5… GO." This sheet lists every single cue in order so nothing is missed. LX = Lighting. SX = Sound. Production Schedule
Made by: Produser + Stage Manager · Distribute to ALL departments on Day 1.
Key rule: The Director's moodboard and the locked script must be done in Week 1 before anything else. All other departments work in parallel from Week 2 onwards. Do not let departments wait for each other — work at the same time.
⚡ WEEK 1 — Foundation Week (Do this first or everything delays)
Day 1–2
Director: create moodboard + character vision sheetScript Writer: finalize and lock the script (no more changes after this)Producer: distribute script + moodboard to all departments
Day 3–4
Wardrobe: begin sourcing Bandung suit, Mama kebaya, Jin costumesProps: make complete props list and begin sourcingSound & Lighting: read cue sheet draft, test equipmentPublication: start poster design using moodboard
Day 5–7
Stage Manager: create rehearsal schedule and master cue sheetDirector: create stageboard (blocking maps)Finance: collect receipts, approve purchases
📋 WEEK 2 — Rehearsal Week (Scenes blocked and learned)
Day 8–9
Full read-through rehearsal with all actors (script in hand is okay)Director: walk through blocking from stageboard with cast
Day 10–11
Rehearse Scene 1 full (blocking + lines)Wardrobe: fitting sessions for all priority costumesProps: all props sourced and labeled
Day 12–14
Rehearse Scene 2 and Scene 3 fullSound & Lighting: test all cues with empty stagePublication: poster finalized and distributed
🔧 WEEK 3 — Technical Week (Everything comes together)
Day 15–16
Rehearsal with ALL props on stage (no substitutes)Sound & Lighting: run cue sheet with live actors for first time
Day 17–18
Full dress rehearsal in costume — full show, no stopsStage Manager calls all cues for the first time in real timeDirector gives notes after. All departments fix issues.
Day 19
Final dress rehearsal — treat exactly like show dayNo notes during — notes given only after it ends
Day 20
Rest day. Review notes. Last-minute fixes only. No major changes.

Finance Rules for Beginners
1. No receipt = no reimbursement. Take a photo of every receipt immediately.
2. Approve first, buy second. Any purchase must be cleared with Finance before spending.
3. Borrow before buying. Ask if someone can lend the item — props, costumes, equipment.
4. Keep a contingency fund. Reserve 10–15% of total budget for surprise expenses.
5. Final report after show. Finance submits a full spending summary to the Producer post-show.
